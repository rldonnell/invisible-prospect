# P5 Marketing — Services & Programs Catalog

These are the services and programs P5 Marketing offers to clients. When building a client brief, cross-reference `references/client-context.md`, recent Gmail threads, Fireflies transcripts, and Slack discussions to determine which services each client is actively subscribed to.

## Core Programs

### Search ID (SEO)
Search engine optimization program. Includes technical SEO audits, keyword research, content strategy, on-page optimization, backlink building, and monthly reporting. Tracked via Ahrefs/DataForSEO for domain rating, organic traffic, and keyword rankings.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings discussing keywords, backlinks, content optimization, site audits, indexability, ranking, SERP, schema markup.

### Intent ID
Intent data identification program. Uses Audience Lab to identify high-intent prospects from behavioral data and third-party intent signals. Delivers targeted contact lists for outreach campaigns.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning Audience Lab, intent data, prospect lists, segments, lead lists, intent signals.

### Visitor ID
Website visitor identification program. Uses tracking pixel (V4 pixel) to identify anonymous website visitors by matching IP/device data to contact records. Feeds into email sequences and retargeting workflows.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning visitor ID, pixel, website visitors, visitor tracking, V4 pixel, site visitor identification.

### Email Marketing / Drip Sequences
Automated email sequences triggered by visitor identification, intent data, or form submissions. Managed through GoHighLevel, Resend, or Amazon SES depending on client. Includes drip campaigns, nurture sequences, and transactional email.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning email sequence, drip, nurture, GHL workflows, Resend, SES, email automation, send-emails.

### Website Development & Updates
WordPress/Astra Pro website design, development, and ongoing maintenance. Includes new page creation, design updates, content changes, hosting maintenance, plugin updates, and performance optimization.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning website, Astra, WordPress, page build, site update, hosting, CSS, design, migration, Vercel.

### Meta Advertising (Facebook/Instagram Ads)
Paid social media advertising on Meta platforms. Includes campaign setup, audience targeting, creative development, A/B testing, budget management, and performance reporting. Often works in conjunction with Visitor ID for retargeting.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning Meta, Facebook ads, Instagram ads, ad creative, audience targeting, ad spend, promotional dollars, ROAS.

### GoHighLevel (GHL) Management
CRM and marketing automation platform management. Includes workflow setup, pipeline configuration, appointment booking, SMS campaigns, and contact management. Used as the central hub for many client marketing operations.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning GHL, GoHighLevel, workflows, pipeline, CRM, booking, appointments, automations.

### Content Creation
Blog posts, landing pages, social media content, video scripts, and other marketing content. May be AI-assisted using Claude. Includes content calendars and editorial planning.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning content, blog, articles, copywriting, landing page copy, content calendar.

### Cold Outreach (via Instantly)
Cold email outreach campaigns to prospect lists. Managed through Instantly with dedicated sending domains and mailboxes. Typically limited to ~120 emails/day across 2 domains with 4 mailboxes.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning cold email, outreach, Instantly, prospecting, cold outreach, mailbox warmup.

### Hosting & Maintenance
Ongoing website hosting management, security updates, uptime monitoring, SSL certificates, and routine maintenance checks.

Communication signals: Emails or meetings mentioning hosting check, maintenance, SSL, security update.

## Identifying Active Services

When determining which services a client has, use this priority order:
1. **`references/client-context.md`** — the authoritative source listing confirmed services per client
2. **Recent meeting transcripts (Fireflies)** — discussions about specific services indicate active engagement
3. **Email communications (Gmail)** — reports, updates, and requests indicate service delivery
4. **Slack threads** — internal team discussions often reference active work by service type
5. **Google Drive docs** — strategy documents, ICPs, and reports indicate service scope

If a service appears in communications but isn't listed in `client-context.md`, flag it as potentially active but unconfirmed, and suggest Robert update the context file.
